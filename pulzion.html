Pulzion

PULZION 2018

Pulzion is a grand annual flagship event conducted by PASC which includes innovative and interesting technical and non-technical events. Started in 2014, in a stretch of 5 years, Pulzion has grown to a grand event in PICT. The various technical and non-technical events helps the students to grow in all aspects beyond the scope of the syllabus. The technical events are aimed to induce coding culture and competence amongst the students while the non-technical events help to polish their artistic side. It is one of the best opportunities for the students to enhance their skill sets and improve overall personality. Students from all over Pune and also from some other parts of Maharashtra take part in the event. Each year’s event is based on a theme. Pulzion 2018 was based on the theme ‘Time and Space’. Time, as well as space, proves to be extremely critical elements in a computer engineer’s life. The theme hence drew reference to the most important complexities which are essential for an engineer’s existence.

About ACM

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is an international learned society for computing. It was founded in 1947, and is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society. The ACM is a non-profit professional membership group, with more than 100,000 members as of 2011. Its headquarters are in New York City.

Bugg Off

"Programming allows you to think about thinking, and while debugging you learn learning." With the same motto, Bug off is a competition where the coders were expected to get rid of all the bugs from the given set of codes and achieve the desired output in a limited time frame. It consisted of two rounds, in the first round the coders can debug the bugged codes in any order while in the second debugging them in the given order is mandatory. The participant (Individual/Team) with the maximum number of such debugged codes was declared as the winner.

Code exact

A coding competition based on perfection along with speed. To add to the flair of the event a wildcard round was also conducted and boosted the winners from each category to directly reach the final round. The contest was conducted in two rounds, Round 1 was based on logical and technical questions on C and C++ while round 2 tested the codes written against preliminary test cases during the contest. The team with the maximum number of perfect codes was declared as the winner.

Just coding

Just Coding made the coders crack their brain really hard while writing correct and efficient codes for the given set of problems statements. The event consisted of two rounds, in the first one the coders had to code for given problems in a limited time frame, while in the second one the difficulty of the problems was raised to test the competence of the coders. The participant (Individual/Team) with the maximum number of efficient and correct codes was declared as the winner.

Code Buddy

A coding competition where the coder was expected to understand, analyze and complete his buddy’s code within the given time frame. In the first round, the coders were expected to code 10 easy questions within 30 minutes. Round 2 was a relay coding round where coders were to solve 2 different problem sets by swapping with their partner in the given intervals. The team with the maximum score was declared as the winner.

Photoshop Royale

An event where designs spoke for themselves, Photoshop Royale brought out the best digital imagery skills out of all the digital artists. The theme of the poster for the first round was ‘Time and Space’, where the artists designed exceptionally creative, ingenious and meaningful posters. The top 10 artists from the first round were eligible for the second round where they faced more fierce competition, facilitated by the theme being given on the spot. After an intense judgment by the judges, the best one was declared the winner.

Data Quest

Data Quest was one of its kind data science competitions which took place for the first time in Pune. The participants were tested upon their data science and machine learning skills to optimize the given metric for the given data set.

Web and Android Dev

This event was the perfect opportunity for developers to showcase their talent by making creative, unique and appealing websites or android applications in accordance with the problem statements provided. The problem statements were released a week before the event and the participants had to upload their developed app on Github and share the link.

Electroquest

Electro Quest was an electrified and an exhilarating treasure hunt across the entire campus, wherein the clues were to be decoded using EnTC related principles and fundamentals. The qualifying teams were then given problem statements having difficulty level as per the current year of participants. For the second round, the participants had to face simple circuit debugging and circuit building problems. The winner was decided depending on the accuracy and the speed of completing the given tasks in both the rounds.

Dextrous

With the aim of giving the first-hand experience to the students regarding the corporate pre-placement interview process, Dextrous provided the students with lifelong experience. The candidates were subjected to a standard interviewing procedure comprising an Aptitude Test and a Group discussion followed by a personal Interview. The winner was decided on the basis of his/her combined performance in all the three rounds.

Fandom Quiz

Catering to the needs of all the series maniacs, fandom quiz had a quiz on fandom topics like Game of Thrones, Harry Potter, FRIENDS, Marvel and DC. Round 1 witnessed a general quiz of 20 minutes duration, while Round 2 was a buzzer round. Also, a debate on the most famous topic Marvel versus DC was held, which received a lot of exhilarating and positive response from both participants as well as the audience.

Cerebro

Aimed to quench the thirst of logicians, Cerebro presented them the best opportunity to test their logical and analytical skills. The question was framed in such a way to judge the wit of the participant. The competition was held in two phases, Prelim and Final. Both had similar question paper pattern with different levels of difficulty. The candidate who qualified prelims had to appear for the finals and the candidate with the maximum score in finals won the contest.

Insight

Let your thoughts fly and imaginations wander. With this motto, in Insight the budding writers gave birth to their own piece of literature i.e. paragraph, a story or a poem, related to the image displayed. The writers unfurled their imaginations to the extreme and orchestrated a splendid art of writing. The most creative and unique piece of writing was declared as the winner.

Quiz to Bid

Quiz to Bid, was the perfect chance for all the cricket enthusiasts to create the best team using the strategic building and mastered bidding. The first round was a general quiz based on cricket and the second round saw a full-fledged real-time auction, just like the IPL auctions, an enthralling experience for the participants.

Workshop

With great advancement, comes its negative influences.Today, in this highly technologized world, everybody faces the effect of growing number of system vulnerabilities. And to tackle the same, The Ethical Hacking Workshop, conducted by CYBERCURE Solutions and organised by the PICT ACM Student Chapter, prepared the participants to sustain in spite of these threats. The workshop was organized on 25th and 26th of August.The workshop was blessed by the presence of Mr. Sangeet Chopra, who is among the top 10 hackers of India, as our speaker.
